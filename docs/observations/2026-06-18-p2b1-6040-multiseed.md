# Observation: Grounding-Heavier Mix Lifts POPE's Mean but Not Its Variance (P2-B1, multi-seed)

**Date:** 2026-06-18
**Context:** The seeded variance test showed POPE is a coin-flip at the 50/50 COCO-grounding+ScienceQA mix (bal-acc 5–62, ~half above floor) while MMBench is robust. This run tests the obvious lever — **shift toward grounding (60/40)** — across **3 seeds**, judged on the worst-case/median POPE, to see whether more grounding makes POPE *reliably* above floor while MMBench stays up. This is also the first properly-seeded test of the 60/40 ratio (the earlier 60/40 was the discarded unseeded run).
**Verdict:** **Partial.** More grounding clearly raises POPE's *central tendency* (median 13→48), but does **not** make it reliably above floor (still 1/3 of seeds; median just under 50) and the **variance persists** (~40-pt spread). MMBench stays above floor but drops (less ScienceQA). The ratio moves the mean, not the variance — implicating capacity/stability, not data balance, as the real limiter.

---

## Result (floor-adjusted, 3 seeds each; spec `55f7a804`, 60/40 = 1386 grounding + 928 ScienceQA, rank 16, 3 epochs)

| Mix | POPE bal-acc (seeds 1/2/3) | median | above floor | MMBench (median) | RealWorldQA |
|---|---|---:|:---:|---:|---|
| 50/50 (`151cf686`) | 62.3 / 13.3 / 5.0 | 13.3 | 1/3 | 0.59 (0.59–0.70, all ✓) | ~floor |
| **60/40 (`55f7a804`)** | 29.5 / 48.3 / 68.9 | **48.3** | **1/3** | 0.55 (0.55–0.58, all ✓) | ~floor |

## Reading

- **Grounding weight lifts POPE — confirmed cleanly.** Median POPE 13.3 → 48.3 and minimum 5 → 29.5 going 50/50 → 60/40. The direction is real (more grounding helps POPE), and it retroactively confirms the earlier unseeded "60/40 → POPE 13" was an unlucky draw, not a lever effect (the true 60/40 POPE distribution is 29–69).
- **But POPE is still not reliable.** Only 1 of 3 seeds clears the 50 floor; the median (48) is *just below* it; and the spread is still ~40 points. The variance — not just the mean — is the problem, and 10pp more grounding did not shrink it.
- **MMBench paid a modest price.** 0.59–0.70 → 0.55–0.58 (still all above floor) — the cost of cutting ScienceQA from 1386 to 928 rows. The POPE↔MMBench tradeoff is real and the grounding cache caps at 1386, so pushing much further grounding-heavy starves MMBench.

## The deeper signal — variance, not ratio, is the wall

Across both mixes, the POPE *spread* stays wide (~40–57 pts) regardless of ratio; the ratio only shifts the center. That is the signature of a **capacity / training-stability** limit, not a data-balance one: at 0.5B with a from-scratch projector and batch-1, the model lands on very different yes/no calibrations run to run, and the mix can bias the average but not stabilise it. Data ratio is a mean-shifter; it is not a variance-reducer here.

## What the system does next (a fork — human decision)

1. **Push the ratio + report median-of-3** — try ~65/35 (frac ~0.54) purely to see if POPE's *median* clears floor; cheap, but expected to cost MMBench further and unlikely to fix the variance. A data-only patch.
2. **Attack variance directly** — the more promising direction: (a) **capacity** (0.5B → a larger LM, which breaks the ≤450M edge budget and is therefore a Phase-3 / budget-relaxation question); (b) **training stability** — warm-start/seed the projector better, longer/converged alignment, or batch >1 (M-series memory permitting) to reduce init sensitivity. These target the actual wall.
3. **Accept per-axis single-skill students** — ship `d3423bc0`-style grounding and `b2feb6b1`-style MMBench separately (each reliable on its axis), and report the multi-skill student honestly as "achievable but variance-limited at 0.5B."

## Status of the multi-skill goal

No mix tested so far gives **one student reliably above ≥2 floors across seeds**. MMBench-from-matched-data is reliable; POPE-in-the-mix is not. The Goals "competitive on ≥2 benchmarks with one model" bar is **not met robustly** at 0.5B — it is gated on the variance/capacity problem above, not on data.
